Activity Patterns in Male vs Female Mice

How does average activity over time differ between male and female mice, and how is that affected by the estrus cycle?

This visualization is based on the Mouse Data dataset, which tracks core body temperature and activity every minute for two weeks ‚Äî totaling over 500,000 rows of data. Each mouse follows a 12-hour light / 12-hour dark cycle, and because mice are nocturnal, most of their activity occurs during the dark phase.

A key biological difference is the 4-day estrus cycle in female mice. During estrus, females tend to be more active and warmer, especially around ovulation. These changes are visualized here, letting you explore both gender-based differences and cyclical behavior across time.

Use the controls below to:

  • Filter between male and female mice
  • Switch between activity and temperature
  • View normalized averages or individual patterns
  • Zoom into specific days or time windows

Blue corresponds to male mice, and pink to female mice.

Design Decisions

Why did we incorporate our interactive features and their design?

  • Line charts: Show precise changes in activity and temperature at minute resolution. Allow users to trace patterns over time and spot recurring peaks linked to the estrus cycle. Enable zooming for closer inspection.
  • Male/Female toggles: Make comparisons clearer by removing overlapping lines. Help focus on one group at a time when differences are subtle.
  • Activity vs. Temperature toggle: Reveals how movement correlates with body temperature across light/dark cycles. Reduces the need for separate charts.
  • Data normalization: Raw values vary too much across mice. Scaling everything to 0‚Äì100% lets users compare patterns without baseline differences skewing the chart.
  • Tooltips: Help users find exact values without adding extra labels or cluttering the interface.
  • Zoom & pan: Data is dense‚Äîzooming helps isolate specific days or phases like estrus. Panning gives flexibility across the full two-week span.
  • Estrus markers: Visually align behavior and temperature spikes with ovulation cycles. Reinforce the biological context behind the data.

We considered alternatives like small multiples and area charts but found them too cluttered or misleading. This layout emphasizes clarity, flexibility, and cycle visibility.
